TNIIC

Tamil Nadu Ilam Innovators Challenge 2019 is a first of its kind initiative taken, as per the Tamil Nadu Startup and Innovation Policy (2018-23), by EDII-TN and School Education Department, GoTN partnering with Startups in the field of Education and Ed-Tech - Infinite Engineers, Warhorse, MADIEE Games, Getset Ventures, E21 IIT-Madras and SP Robotic Works Maker Lab.

In the words of our late beloved former President of India, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, “All of us are not born with the same talent, but all of us are given an equal opportunity to develop our talents”, TNIIC 2019 focusses on developing the 21st Century Skills - 4Cs’ - Creativity, Communication, Collaboration and Critical Thinking. These 21st Century Skills will spearhead the students towards the path of becoming future innovators and entrepreneurs.


The students will be trained in concepts of Growth Mindset, Ethos,Pathos & Logos, Design Thinking, Business Planning and Entrepreneurship and the art of Pitching their ideas. Their Science Projects will no longer just be for Science Fairs and Exhibitions, with this platform they can explore the possibilities of becoming an entrepreneur in the future and make sure that their science projects reach and help the people.

(More than 50% participation of Government and Government aided Schools will be and has been ensured)

MOU between TANSIM/EDII-TN and School Education Department

A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) was signed between TANSIM/EDII-TN and the School Education Department, Government of Tamil Nadu on 27.06.2019 (International MSME Day).

  • To Plan and Organize the Tamil Nadu Ilam Innovators Challenge in all the Educational districts of Tamil Nadu in order to inculcate the spirit of innovation and entrepreneurial skills in school students.
  • To create awareness about Makerspaces and Atal Tinkering Labs, help in setting up and maintenance.
  • To train the teachers in understanding the Startup culture, Innovation and Entrepreneurship and modernize the teaching methods.
  • To create, set up and maintain Junior Entrepreneurship Cells (E-Cells) in schools/makerspaces and establish a learning/mentoring relationship with College E-Cells
  • And all other programmes that benefit the students and teachers.

Train The Trainers (TTT)

TRAIN THE TRAINERS (T.T.T) Programme, which initiates the First Phase (May – July, 2019) of the Tamil Nadu Ilam Innovators Challenge 2019, was conceptualized to develop a nurturing relationship between Schools and Colleges.

The College Students were trained by the partnering Startups – Infinite Engineers and Warhorse, on how to mentor school students and facilitate Stage 1 of TNIIC 2019 – District Level Selections of School Students in each of the 32 Districts across the State.

The T.T.T Programme was conducted in 5 Hubs – A Total of 260 College Students were trained, out of which 104 College Students were selected as District Captains for their respective Districts. Stage 1 of TNIIC 2019 was planned to be conducted as 52 Batches in 32 Districts, which means each batch will be facilitated by 2 District Captains.

Stages of TNIIC

STAGE 1 - DISTRICT LEVEL SELECTIONS

The District Level Selections of School Students has been completed successfully during August - November 2019 in all the 32 districts of Tamil Nadu, with the support of the School Education Department (Under the guidance of Th. M.Vasu, Joint Director (NSS) and Chief Education Officers (CEOs) of each District), IEDP Hubs of EDII-TN and the District Captains.

The Training Kits for District Captains and School Students were provided by the Infinite Engineers Team. Registrations were made online through www.tniic.in and the Principals/Headmasters of each School have been requested to select 3 Students from their school, who have shown signs of creativity in class rather than the ones who get the first 3 ranks.

52 Batches were conducted during this Second Phase of Tamil Nadu Ilam Innovators Challenge. 1471 students (494 Teams) and 496 teachers from 494 schools were trained 21st Century Skills - 4Cs - Communication, Collaboration, Creativity and Critical Thinking through a Gamified Module developed by the partnering Startups, who are the knowledge partners.

Out of 1471 students i.e. 494 Teams, 468 best performing students (159 Teams) were selected by the District Captains for Stage 2 - Region/Hub Level - 4 Day Residential Training Workshops based on their score in the Gamified Module.

More than 120 Atal Tinkering Labs were surveyed during this programme and the comprehensive report of the status with recommendations was submitted to the School Education Department

STAGE 2 - FOUR DAY RESIDENTIAL TRAINING WORKSHOPS

Out of 1471 students i.e. 494 Teams, 468 best performing students (159 Teams) were selected by the District Captains for 2 - Region/Hub Level - 4 Day Residential Training Workshops in 5 Zones; Chennai, Coimbatore, Madurai, Tirunelveli and Trichy.

The Participating Students were split into 2 Batches and the 4 Day Residential Training Workshop was conducted as follows:

Day 1 – @Warhorse - Growth Mindset Workshop – Communication Skills and Growth Mindset – Student’s Favourite - Cup Song to learn about Chunking, Neuroplasticity and how easy it is to enable a growth mindset.

@ MADIEE Games - Ethos, Pathos and Logos Workshop – Developing your Credibility, Logic & Intelligence, Empathy, Teamwork and Problem Solving.

Day 2 - @Infinite Engineers - Design Thinking Workshop – Problem Identification, Ideation, Prototyping,Testing and Problem Solving.

@ E21 and @Getset Ventures - Introduction to Innovation & Entrepreneurship – Ideation,Mind Mapping Techniques, Research, Defining Problem Statements, Business Model Canvas, Art of Pitch Presentation.

Day 3 - @SP Robotics Maker Lab - Robotics Hands-on Session and How technology can be used to solve modern problems and Mentoring Sessions.

Day 4 - Final Pitch through Chart Presentations that includes their Idea /and Business Model Canvas(BMC) for their idea / innovation.
